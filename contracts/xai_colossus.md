# xAI Colossus 1 + 2 — Memphis / Southaven Self-Build

## TL;DR

xAI's Memphis/Southaven Colossus footprint is a self-build / affiliate-owned AI training campus, not a third-party take-or-pay capacity contract. xAI's primary disclosures support a 200,000-GPU supercomputer with stated path to 1 million GPUs in Memphis and **"over one million H100 GPU equivalents" across Colossus I and II by year-end 2025** (Series E announcement, January 6, 2026). Local Epoch carries Colossus 1 at 0.425 GW facility operational plus 0.017 GW remaining, and Colossus 2 at 1.494 GW facility full buildout by 2026-05-13 — total Epoch-attributed Colossus capacity is **1.936 GW**. The local overlay's `xai_colossus2_residual` row of **0.078 GW facility / 0.060 GW IT (T6)** is an analyst residual after subtracting Epoch's full Colossus 1 + 2 buildout (1.936 GW) from Elon Musk's "almost 2GW" X-post claim (Tom's Hardware, December 30, 2025): 2.000 − 1.936 ≈ 0.064 GW IT, rounded to 0.060 GW IT × PUE 1.30 = 0.078 GW facility. **Tom's Hardware is a secondary source quoting an X post; no primary xAI release with MW disclosure exists.** MLGW grid-service evidence (Paul Lowery 150 MW May 2025 plus 150 MW pending) and Mississippi/Shelby permit/litigation evidence frame the binding power and regulatory risks.

## Counterparties

- **Operator:** xAI Corp. (Elon Musk-affiliated). Self-build at 3231 Riverport Rd / Paul R. Lowry Road (Colossus 1) and 5420 Tulane Rd, Memphis, TN (Colossus 2). MZX Tech LLC operates the Southaven, MS gas plant at 2875 Stanton Road per MDEQ permit.
- **Anchor tenant:** xAI itself (training compute for Grok and downstream xAI workloads). Not a third-party customer contract.
- **Financing partners:**
  - **xAI Series E $20B** announced January 6, 2026 (xAI primary release).
  - Behind-the-meter generation: Solar SMT-130 turbine packages (15-turbine permit conversion to 12-turbine via Shelby County notice April 2026); MZX Tech 41-turbine, 1,200 MW permitted gas plant at Southaven per MDEQ PSD construction permit (2026-01-15).
  - Utility partner: Memphis Light, Gas and Water (MLGW) for grid service to Paul Lowery; TVA upstream.

## Risk Axes

1. **Counterparty:** xAI is private; no SEC disclosures, no take-or-pay, no third-party customer at site level. Series E $20B (January 2026) supports infrastructure buildout generally; no Colossus-specific project finance found. Disclosure quality is the binding counterparty risk vector.
2. **Regulatory:** **Shelby County permit 01156-01PC** (Scribd mirror, July 2, 2025) authorized 15 Solar SMT-130 turbines at 16.48 MW each; the **April 7, 2026 Shelby County public notice** requests conversion of the construction permit to an operating permit and reduction from 15 to 12 turbines. The **NAACP/YGG appeal filing (July 16, 2025)** alleges 35 turbines and more than 420 MW generating capacity at peak, challenging permit logic. The **MDEQ Southaven PSD construction permit (2026-01-15)** covers MZX Tech LLC at 2875 Stanton Road for a 41-turbine, 1,200 MW permitted gas plant. The **NAACP federal complaint (April 14, 2026)** alleges 27 unpermitted turbines and that the Southaven Colossus Gas Plant "supplies power to xAI's Colossus 2." Permit appeals and federal litigation are active.
3. **Power / interconnect:** **MLGW May 5, 2025 update** says xAI's Paul Lowery (Colossus 1) site "is receiving 150MW of power" from TVA/MLGW grid with a possible second 150 MW pending TVA approval and substation upgrades. **MLGW July 22, 2025 Tulane statement** says MLGW is "not supplying power to their supercomputer" for Colossus 2 at 5420 Tulane Road, with general service of approximately 0.5 MW only. Behind-the-meter turbines and Southaven gas generation are critical to operations; grid service alone does not explain Epoch facility-MW estimates.
4. **Supply chain:** Local Epoch full-buildout estimate implies roughly 550k GPUs / 1.39M H100e at Colossus 2; delivery depends on advanced NVIDIA H100/H200/B200/GB200/Blackwell systems plus Solar SMT-130 turbines, batteries, dry coolers/condensers. xAI's "over one million H100 GPU equivalents" claim spans Colossus I and II.
5. **Technology obsolescence:** Fast GPU, cooling, battery, and turbine integration creates reliability and refresh-cycle risk. Cooling architecture and water-recycling buildout (originally 13 MGD water recycling facility) sequenced after Colossus 2 stabilization per AOL/Commercial Appeal April 9, 2026 reporting.
6. **Financing:** xAI Series E $20B supports broad infrastructure; no Colossus 2 project-finance package found. No site-level financial transparency.
7. **Structural optionality:** xAI's GPU/H100e claims, Musk's 2 GW claim, and local Epoch MW estimates do not share a single disclosed power basis. **The residual atom is an analyst inference, not a disclosed MW commitment** — kept at T6 to reflect this.
